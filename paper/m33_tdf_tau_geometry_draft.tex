\begin{abstract}
We use the nearby spiral galaxy M33 as an intermediate-scale test of whether the missing acceleration inferred from its rotation curve can be represented as a smooth, low-parameter time-delay field ($\tau$) geometry, and whether the same reconstructed $\tau$-map can generate deflection-\emph{proxy} predictions without introducing a separate halo component or a lensing-only $\tau$ fit.
Using the Corbelli et al.\ (2014) rotation sample and a documented baryonic decomposition (PASS\_WITH\_CAVEAT), we compare baryonic-only, NFW, and Burkert baselines to low-parameter $\tau$-gradient knot models.
On the standard fit mask ($0.4$--$23$\,kpc, $n{=}56$), the three-knot TDF model achieves RMSE $\approx 3.51$\,km\,s$^{-1}$ and AIC $\approx 63.0$, competitive with the NFW baseline (AIC $\approx 68.0$).
An axisymmetric two-dimensional $\tau$-map and sky-plane projection are built from the frozen radial solution; a normalized deflection proxy (not arcseconds) follows from the same map.
A dynamical upper-bound consistency check against L\'opez Fune et al.\ (2017) enclosed mass at $\sim 23$\,kpc yields PASS\_WITH\_CAVEAT.
We do \emph{not} claim that dark matter is disproven, that TDF replaces $\Lambda$CDM, or that lensing has been observationally confirmed; physical lensing calibration and M33 weak-lensing comparison remain future work.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Rotation curves of disk galaxies require mass components beyond visible baryons in standard interpretations.
M33 offers a nearby laboratory where gas-rich kinematics and literature halo models are well documented \citep{Corbelli2014}.
The time-delay field (TDF) framework posits that the ``missing'' acceleration can be organized as a radial $\tau$-geometry coupled to baryons through a low-parameter gradient model, rather than introducing an independent dark-matter halo in the TDF analysis branch.

This work is deliberately scoped: we test \emph{consistency} of a frozen $\tau$ reconstruction with rotation data and with a \emph{normalized} deflection proxy derived from the same map.
We do not claim cosmological validation, dark-matter replacement, or observational lensing detection.

\section{Baryonic decomposition and caveats}
\label{sec:baryons}

Gas and stellar disk contributions follow the documented axisymmetric pipeline.
Fig.~12 digitized curves are sanity checks only, not canonical velocities.
All halo and TDF fits inherit the baryonic caveat: offsets of order $\sim 7$--$10$\,km\,s$^{-1}$ may appear at some radii in spot checks.

\section{Baseline models}
\label{sec:baselines}

\textbf{Baryonic-only.} On the fit mask, RMSE $\approx 58.6$\,km\,s$^{-1}$ and $\chi^2 \approx 2.0 \times 10^4$ (Table~\ref{tab:models}), demonstrating that baryons alone do not explain the observed rotation curve in this processed dataset.

\textbf{NFW.} Two-parameter NFW halo on D1 baryons: RMSE $\approx 4.16$\,km\,s$^{-1}$, reduced $\chi^2 \approx 1.21$, AIC $\approx 68.0$ --- treated as a $\Lambda$CDM \emph{comparison baseline}, not as proof of TDF.

\textbf{Burkert.} RMSE $\approx 23.0$\,km\,s$^{-1}$; core radius often at the upper fit bound ($r_0 \approx 200$\,kpc) --- \emph{boundary-limited} in this dataset; not used as a preferred anchor.

\section{TDF radial reconstruction}
\label{sec:tdf-radial}

Phases~3A--3B reconstruct $\tau$-gradients from $\Delta v^2 = v_{\mathrm{obs}}^2 - v_{\mathrm{bar}}^2$ with fixed $K_\tau{=}1$ (normalization convention, not physical calibration).
Direct and regularized reconstructions are diagnostic; formal AIC/BIC comparison uses Phase~3C knot models only.

\section{Low-parameter TDF comparison}
\label{sec:tdf-compare}

We fit $\tau$-gradient knot values (3, 4, and 5 knots) with $K_\tau$ fixed.
The three-knot model (\texttt{tdf\_lowparam\_3knot}) attains RMSE $\approx 3.51$\,km\,s$^{-1}$, $\chi^2 \approx 57.0$, AIC $\approx 63.0$, and BIC $\approx 69.0$.
The five-knot model has the lowest RMSE ($\approx 3.35$\,km\,s$^{-1}$) but a higher BIC penalty ($\approx 76.5$).
Relative to NFW, the three-knot TDF model is \emph{competitive} on AIC/BIC on this mask; this is rotation-dynamics consistency, not evidence against dark matter.

\section{Sensitivity and stability}
\label{sec:sensitivity}

Phase~3D audits knot count, $K_\tau$ sweeps, smoothing, and fit-mask variants.
The three-knot model beats NFW on AIC at the default mask; ranking is \emph{caveated} under stricter masks and documents $K_\tau$ scale degeneracy (max RMSE spread $\approx 0.42$\,km\,s$^{-1}$ across $K_\tau$ sweep).
See \texttt{outputs/tables/phase3d\_tdf\_sensitivity\_summary.csv}.

\section{Deflection proxy and upper-bound consistency}
\label{sec:lensing}

Phase~5A computes a \textbf{normalized\_proxy} deflection field from the sky $\tau$-map ($\alpha_\tau$ scale $=1$; no arcsecond calibration).
This is a prediction \emph{scaffold}, not observational lensing confirmation.
No separate halo is added; $\tau$ is not refit for lensing.

Phase~5C-B compares a Newtonian-equivalent enclosed-mass proxy $M_{\tau,\mathrm{eff}} = r\,v_\tau^2/G$ to L\'opez Fune et al.\ (2017) Burkert $M_{\mathrm{enclosed}}$ at $\sim 23$\,kpc ($6.7 \pm 1.2 \times 10^{10}\,M_\odot$).
At $r{=}22.72$\,kpc, $M_{\tau,\mathrm{eff}} \approx 7.5 \times 10^{10}\,M_\odot$ (ratio $\approx 1.12$) --- \textbf{PASS\_WITH\_CAVEAT} within the quoted upper envelope.
This is a \emph{dynamical} upper-bound consistency check with partial circularity (Corbelli inputs); it is \textbf{not} weak lensing.

\section{Limitations}
\label{sec:limits}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Single galaxy; D1-derived baryonic velocities (PASS\_WITH\_CAVEAT).
  \item $K_\tau{=}1$ is a normalization convention; Phase~3D documents scale degeneracy.
  \item Burkert baseline hits parameter bounds; do not over-interpret.
  \item 2D map is axisymmetric; no independent spiral-arm $\tau$ fit.
  \item Deflection remains \texttt{normalized\_proxy}; physical calibration deferred.
  \item No M33-specific weak-lensing map in repository (\texttt{m33\_direct\_weak\_lensing\_gap}).
  \item Dark matter is \textbf{not disproven}; NFW remains the $\Lambda$CDM reference.
\end{itemize}

\section{Future work}
\label{sec:future}

Physical $\alpha_\tau$ calibration to arcseconds; independent weak-lensing data; improved baryonic systematics; multi-galaxy tests.

\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

M33 supports the \emph{hypothesis-level} statement that a smooth, low-parameter $\tau$-geometry can reconstruct missing rotation acceleration competitively with an NFW baseline on a documented processed dataset, and that the same frozen $\tau$-map propagates to a normalized deflection proxy without a separate halo.
L\'opez Fune dynamical mass provides a caveated upper-bound consistency check only.
Stronger lensing claims require calibration and data beyond this pipeline.
